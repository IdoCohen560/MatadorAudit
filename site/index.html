What is proxy discrimination?

Many AI systems do not use race as an input, but they use variables that correlate with race, like zip code or high school ranking. This means the system can effectively "know" a student's ethnicity without ever seeing it directly. The bars below show how strongly each variable correlates with race. Longer red bars = stronger proxy = higher risk of hidden bias.

How We Measure Fairness

Transparent methodology grounded in established fairness research and legal standards.

Demographic Parity Gap

Measures the difference in positive outcome rates between the most and least favored demographic groups. Based on Dwork et al., "Fairness through Awareness" (ITCS 2012).

Gap = max(group_rate) - min(group_rate)

A gap of 0 means perfect parity. The larger the gap, the greater the disparity between groups.

< 0.08 = Low Risk 0.08 - 0.15 = Medium > 0.15 = High Risk
Disparate Impact Ratio (Four-Fifths Rule)

Compares the selection rate of the least-favored group to the most-favored group. Based on the EEOC "four-fifths rule" established in Griggs v. Duke Power Co. (1971) and codified in the Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures.

Ratio = min(group_rate) / max(group_rate)

A ratio below 0.80 (80%) is considered evidence of potential adverse impact under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

≥ 0.80 = Passing 0.60 - 0.80 = Concern < 0.60 = Failing
Equalized Odds

Measures whether the true positive rate (and false positive rate) are equal across demographic groups. A system satisfies equalized odds when it performs equally well for all groups, not just overall. Based on Hardt, Price & Srebro, "Equality of Opportunity in Supervised Learning" (NeurIPS 2016).

EO Gap = max(TPR_group) - min(TPR_group)

This is especially important for decisions that affect opportunities: financial aid, admissions, and course recommendations.

Proxy Discrimination Detection

Uses point-biserial correlation (Pearson, 1909) to measure the relationship between each feature and protected demographic membership. Even when race is not used as an input, variables like zip code, high school ranking, or "predicted persistence" scores can act as proxies. Based on Datta, Tschantz & Datta, "Automated Experiments on Ad Privacy Settings" (PoPETs 2015).

r_pb = (M1 - M0) / S * sqrt(n1 * n0 / N^2)

A variable with a strong correlation to a protected attribute is a potential proxy, meaning the system may effectively "know" a student's race through supposedly neutral data.

< 0.15 = Low 0.15 - 0.25 = Medium > 0.25 = High Proxy Risk

Claude AI is used solely for generating natural-language fairness reports from aggregate statistical summaries. No student records or personally identifiable information are transmitted to any external API. All fairness metrics are computed locally using our own implementation.

Methodology References

Our fairness metrics are based on peer-reviewed research and established legal standards:

Demographic Parity

Dwork, C., Hardt, M., Pitassi, T., Reingold, O., & Zemel, R. (2012). "Fairness through Awareness." Innovations in Theoretical Computer Science (ITCS). ACM.

Disparate Impact / Four-Fifths Rule

Equal Employment Opportunity Commission. (1978). Uniform Guidelines on Employee Selection Procedures, 29 C.F.R. Part 1607. Legal precedent: Griggs v. Duke Power Co., 401 U.S. 424 (1971).

Equalized Odds

Hardt, M., Price, E., & Srebro, N. (2016). "Equality of Opportunity in Supervised Learning." Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 29.

Proxy Discrimination Detection

Datta, A., Tschantz, M. C., & Datta, A. (2015). "Automated Experiments on Ad Privacy Settings." Proceedings on Privacy Enhancing Technologies (PoPETs), 2015(1). Point-biserial correlation: Pearson, K. (1909).

Impossibility Theorem

Chouldechova, A. (2017). "Fair Prediction with Disparate Impact: A Study of Bias in Recidivism Prediction Instruments." Big Data, 5(2), 153-163. Establishes that certain fairness metrics are mutually exclusive.

The Problem

Universities deploy AI for admissions, financial aid, course recommendations, and plagiarism detection. Nobody checks if these systems treat all students fairly. Accuracy metrics can mask severe disparities across demographic groups.

The Gap

Existing fairness tools require Python expertise. The people who need auditing most department chairs, financial aid directors, admissions officers cannot use them. MatadorAudit bridges this gap with a no-code dashboard.
